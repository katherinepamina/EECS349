Predicting Song Hotttnesss

In which we predict a song's popularity by analyzing its metadata.

EECS 349: Machine Learning
Northwestern University
Professor Downey

Introduction

Our task is to predict a song's "hotttnesss" given its duration, energy, key, loudness, mode, tempo, danceability, year, and time signature. The ability to predict a song's popularity is important because it has direct applications in music production and analyzing pop music trends. Our results may have implications for what it takes for a song to become a hit, or whether a song's success can be predicted at all. Data

The dataset we have been using consists of a subset of the Million Song Dataset. The attributes we used are duration (numeric), energy (numeric), key (nominal), loudness (numeric), mode (nominal), and tempo (numeric). Song_hotttnesss is given as a numeric attribute. We have 10,000 random examples chosen from the Million Song Dataset. The full sample will be used for development, and we plan on using 40/40/20% for training/validation/testing.
